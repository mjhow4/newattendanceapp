About CourtPal

CourtPal is a Court Calendar System that allows direct communication between The District Attorney, Defense Attorney, and the Court Clerk.
Members of the Public are also allow a limited view of CourtPal, which includes items that are customarily included on a Court Calendar in addition
to the PAL System updated in real time.

The PAL System

PAL stands for Present, Absent, or Late. As the District Attorney is conducting Calendar Call the District Attorney can easily check off whether someone is
P - Present, A - Absent, or L - Late. As the Distict Attorney checks off the list for each Defendant, the CourtRoom Clerk, who has access to the same view as
the District Attorney, CourtPal is updated immediately. The Defense Attorney View and the Public Calendar View is also updated immediately but with color codes;
Present = Green, Absent = Red, and Late = Yellow. This allows the Defense Attorney and Public to immediately know if they have been marked Present, Absent, or
Late. This can allow attendance errors to be addressed immediately and those running late to plan accordingly.

Benefits of using CourtPal

All parties involved benefit from the use of CourtPal.

District Attorney

  • Can address easy continuances and easy plea requests prior to Court
  • Direct Communication with Defense Attorney without Office Visits, Phone Calls, Emails, or Fax
  • Can easily check off whether Defendant is PAL (Present, Absent, Late) and easily switch between those options

Defense Attorney

  • Can easily resolve continuances and simple plea requests prior to Court
  • Direct Communication with District Attorney without Office Visits, Phone Calls, Emails, or Fax
  • Can see immediately if client is PAL (Present, Absent, Late)
  • Can prioritize which court rooms to visit based on which client is present versus visiting all court rooms and calling clients to see if they are present
  • Can see immediately if client has Failure to Appear (FTA) and Order For Arrest (OFA) without having to contact or visit the Clerk's Office
  • Fewer distracting office visits and phone calls from clients requesting information that is immediately available on CourtPal such as:
    When is my next Court Date? Do I have an OFA? How much do I owe in Court Costs and Fines?

CourtRoom Clerk

  • Immediate access to District Attorney's entries therefore free to work on other items during Calendar Call
  • Can easily enter Case Disposition directly on CourtPal and is available immediately
  • Fewer distracting visits and phone calls from Attorneys and Pro Se Defendants since key information is immediately avaialble on CourtPal

Public

  • Can freely access CourtPal's Public Calendar View immediately without Login or Registration
  • Can immediately see if their case has been continued
  • If running late for Court can immediately see if they have missed First Calendar Call
  • Instant access to the Official Disposition of their case
  • If Defendant has missed Court, Defendant can view the Calendar and immediately know if there was an OFA issued
